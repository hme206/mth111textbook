Chapter 1: Numbers

Section 1.2: Decimals and Place Value

Learning Objective(s)

  1. Read and write numbers in decimal notation.
  2. Write decimals as fractions.
  3. Write fractions as decimals.

Introduction

In addition to fraction notation, decimal notation is another way to write numbers between 0 and 1. Decimals can also be used to write numbers between any two whole numbers. For example, you may have to write a check for $2,003.38. Or, in measuring the length of a room, you may find that the length is between two whole numbers, such as 35.24 feet. In this section you will focus on reading and writing decimal numbers, and rewriting them in fraction notation.

To read or write numbers written in decimal notation, you need to know the place value of each digit — that is, the value of a digit based on its position within a number. With decimal numbers, the position of a numeral in relation to the decimal point determines its place value. For example, the place value of the 4 in 45.6 is in the tens place, while the place value of 6 in 45.6 is in the tenths place.

Decimal Notation

Decimal numbers are numbers whose place values are based on 10s. Whole numbers are actually decimal numbers that are greater than or equal to zero. The place-value chart can be extended to include numbers less than one, which are sometimes called decimal fractions. A decimal point is used to separate the whole number part of the number from the fraction part.

Suppose you are measuring the length of a driveway and find that it is 745.36 feet. The place values of the decimal part are shown below.

Decimal Numbers Place-Value Chart — 745.36
Hundreds Tens Ones Decimal Point Tenths Hundredths
7 4 5 . 3 6

Notice how the place-value names start from the decimal point. To the left of the decimal point are the ones, tens, and hundreds places — digits that represent whole numbers greater than or equal to zero. To the right of the decimal point are the tenths and hundredths — digits that represent fractional parts less than one.

The place value of a number depends on how far away it is from the decimal point. The chart below shows the digit 4 in seven different place values.

Key Pattern: The "th" Side

The place values to the right of the decimal point all end in "th" (tenths, hundredths, thousandths). There is no such thing as "oneths" — the ones place sits to the left of the decimal point.

Example 1

What is the place value of 8 in 4,279.386?

Solution

Place-Value Chart — 4,279.386
Thousands Hundreds Tens Ones . Tenths Hundredths Thousandths
4 2 7 9 . 3 8 6

The digit 8 is in the hundredths place.

Self Check A

What is the place value of the 7 in 324.2671?

The digit 7 is three decimal places to the right of the decimal point, so it is in the thousandths place.

Reading Decimals

The easiest way to read a decimal number is to read the decimal fraction part as a fraction (without simplifying). For example, 0.4 grams is read as "four tenths of a gram," since the 4 is in the tenths place.

The denominator of the fraction is always a power of ten, and the number of zeros in the denominator equals the number of decimal places to the right of the decimal point.

Decimal Notation Fraction Notation Word Form
0.5 \(\dfrac{5}{10}\) five tenths
0.34 \(\dfrac{34}{100}\) thirty-four hundredths
0.896 \(\dfrac{896}{1{,}000}\) eight hundred ninety-six thousandths

For numbers greater than 1 (mixed numbers), say the whole number part, the word "and" (representing the decimal point), and then the decimal part followed by its place value name.

Decimal Notation Fraction Notation Word Form
9.4 \(9\dfrac{4}{10}\) nine and four tenths
87.49 \(87\dfrac{49}{100}\) eighty-seven and forty-nine hundredths
594.236 \(594\dfrac{236}{1{,}000}\) five hundred ninety-four and two hundred thirty-six thousandths
1,475.3014 \(1{,}475\dfrac{3{,}014}{10{,}000}\) one thousand four hundred seventy-five and three thousand fourteen ten-thousandths

Example 2

Write 0.68 in word form.

Solution

The last decimal place is hundredths, so the denominator is 100 (two zeros, same as the number of decimal places):

\[0.68 = \frac{68}{100} = \text{sixty-eight hundredths}\]

Answer: sixty-eight hundredths.

Example 3

Write 4.379 in word form.

Solution

The last digit (9) is in the thousandths place, so the denominator is 1,000 (three zeros):

\[4.379 = 4\frac{379}{1{,}000}\]

Answer: four and three hundred seventy-nine thousandths.

Self Check B

Write 2.364 in word form.

Two and three hundred sixty-four thousandths. Since \(2.364 = 2\dfrac{364}{1{,}000}\), you read the decimal part as three hundred sixty-four thousandths.

Writing Decimals as Simplified Fractions

To convert a decimal to a fraction, place the digits after the decimal point in the numerator and use the appropriate power of 10 (matching the number of decimal places) as the denominator. Then simplify.

Example 4

Write 0.6 as a simplified fraction.

Solution

The last decimal place is tenths, so use 10 as the denominator:

\[0.6 = \frac{6}{10}\]

Simplify by dividing numerator and denominator by 2:

\[\frac{6}{10} \div \frac{2}{2} = \frac{3}{5}\]

Answer: \(0.6 = \dfrac{3}{5}\)

Example 5

Write 0.64 as a simplified fraction.

Solution

Two decimal places → denominator is 100:

\[0.64 = \frac{64}{100}\]

Simplify by dividing by 4:

\[\frac{64}{100} \div \frac{4}{4} = \frac{16}{25}\]

Answer: \(0.64 = \dfrac{16}{25}\)

Example 6

Write 0.645 as a simplified fraction.

Solution

Three decimal places → denominator is 1,000:

\[0.645 = \frac{645}{1{,}000}\]

Simplify by dividing by 5:

\[\frac{645}{1{,}000} \div \frac{5}{5} = \frac{129}{200}\]

Answer: \(0.645 = \dfrac{129}{200}\)

Example 7

Write 0.007 as a simplified fraction.

Solution

The digit 7 is in the thousandths place, so the denominator is 1,000:

\[0.007 = \frac{7}{1{,}000}\]

This fraction cannot be simplified further.

Answer: \(0.007 = \dfrac{7}{1{,}000}\)

Example 8

Write 8.65 as a simplified mixed fraction.

Solution

Keep the whole number part (8) and convert only the decimal part:

\[8.65 = 8\frac{65}{100}\]

Simplify \(\dfrac{65}{100}\) by dividing by 5:

\[8\frac{65}{100} = 8\frac{13}{20}\]

Answer: \(8.65 = 8\dfrac{13}{20}\)

Self Check C

Write 0.25 as a fraction.

\(0.25 = \dfrac{25}{100} = \dfrac{1}{4}\)

Writing Fractions as Decimals

To write a fraction as a decimal, divide the numerator by the denominator. Use long division if necessary and watch where the decimal point falls in your answer.

If the division produces digits that repeat without end, you have a repeating decimal. Write a bar over the repeating digit(s) to indicate the repetition. For example, \(\dfrac{2}{3} = 0.\overline{6}\).

Example 9

Write \(\dfrac{1}{2}\) as a decimal.

Solution

Divide 1 by 2 using long division:

\[ \begin{array}{r} 0.5 \\[-2pt] 2 \overline{\smash{)}\, 1.0} \\[-2pt] \underline{-\,1.0} \\[-2pt] 0 \end{array} \]

Answer: \(\dfrac{1}{2} = 0.5\)

Example 10

Write \(\dfrac{3}{8}\) as a decimal.

Solution

Divide 3 by 8 using long division:

\[ \begin{array}{r} 0.375 \\[-2pt] 8 \overline{\smash{)}\, 3.000} \\[-2pt] \underline{-\,2.4\phantom{0}} \\[-2pt] 60\phantom{0} \\[-2pt] \underline{-\,56\phantom{0}} \\[-2pt] 40 \\[-2pt] \underline{-\,40} \\[-2pt] 0 \end{array} \]

Answer: \(\dfrac{3}{8} = 0.375\)

Example 11

Convert \(\dfrac{4}{11}\) to a decimal.

Solution

Divide 4 by 11. The pattern 36 repeats indefinitely:

\[ \begin{array}{r} 0.3636\ldots \\[-2pt] 11 \overline{\smash{)}\, 4.0000} \\[-2pt] \underline{-\,3.3\phantom{00}} \\[-2pt] 70\phantom{0} \\[-2pt] \underline{-\,66\phantom{0}} \\[-2pt] 40 \\[-2pt] \underline{-\,33} \\[-2pt] 70 \\[-2pt] \underline{-\,66} \\[-2pt] 4\ldots \end{array} \]

Since 36 repeats, write a bar over both digits:

Answer: \(\dfrac{4}{11} = 0.\overline{36}\)

Example 12

Convert \(2\dfrac{1}{4}\) to a decimal.

Solution

Keep the whole number 2 and convert only the fraction part. Divide 1 by 4:

\[ \begin{array}{r} 0.25 \\[-2pt] 4 \overline{\smash{)}\, 1.00} \\[-2pt] \underline{-\,0.8\phantom{0}} \\[-2pt] 20 \\[-2pt] \underline{-\,20} \\[-2pt] 0 \end{array} \]

Now add the whole number back: \(2 + 0.25 = 2.25\)

Answer: \(2\dfrac{1}{4} = 2.25\)

Tips on Converting Fractions to Decimals

  • Divide the numerator by the denominator.
  • If digits repeat, write a bar over the repeating digit(s): e.g., \(0.\overline{3}\) for 0.333…
  • For mixed numbers, keep the whole number and convert only the fraction part, then combine.

Summary

Decimal notation is another way to write numbers that are less than 1 or that combine whole numbers with decimal fractions. When you write numbers in decimal notation, you can use an extended place-value chart that includes positions for numbers less than one. You can convert between decimal notation and fraction notation in either direction.
