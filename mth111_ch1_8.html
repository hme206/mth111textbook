Chapter 1: Numbers

Section 1.8: Scientific Notation

Learning Objective(s)

  1. Write numbers using scientific notation.
  2. Convert numbers from scientific notation to decimal form.

Introduction

Scientific notation is a specific way to write extremely large or extremely small numbers using exponents. In Britain it is called standard form, but in the US we call it scientific notation. Scientists developed this shorter method to express very large numbers. This notation is based on powers of the base number 10.

Writing Numbers in Scientific Notation

Scientific notation takes a large number like 124,000,000,000 (124 billion) and writes it without all those zeros. To do this we create two parts:

Parts of Scientific Notation

  • The coefficient — a decimal value that must be greater than or equal to 1 but less than 10. We move the decimal point until there is exactly one non-zero digit in front of it.
  • The base — always 10, raised to a power equal to the number of places the decimal point was moved.

A number in scientific notation looks like: \(\text{coefficient} \times 10^{\text{power}}\)

For example, starting with 124,000,000,000, we move the decimal point 11 places to the left to get a coefficient of 1.24. The diagram below shows each step of that movement.

For very small numbers, we use the same process, but because we move the decimal point to the right instead of the left, the exponent is negative.

Convert 102,400,000 into scientific notation.

Solution

The coefficient is 1.024 (drop trailing zeros). The decimal moved 8 places to the left, so the exponent is 8.

\[102{,}400{,}000 = 1.024 \times 10^{8}\]

Example 2

Convert 0.000234 into scientific notation.

Solution

Move the decimal point to the right until one non-zero digit is in front of it:

. 0 0 0 2 3 4
↓
1 ↓
2 ↓
3 ↓
4
Decimal moves 4 places to the right → exponent is −4

The coefficient is 2.34. Because the decimal moved to the right, the exponent is negative.

\[0.000234 = 2.34 \times 10^{-4}\]

Self Check A

Write 120,003,000,000 using scientific notation.

Move the decimal 11 places to the left. The coefficient is 1.20003.

\[120{,}003{,}000{,}000 = 1.20003 \times 10^{11}\]

Self Check B

Write 0.0001204 using scientific notation.

Move the decimal 4 places to the right. Because it moved right, the exponent is negative.

\[0.0001204 = 1.204 \times 10^{-4}\]

Converting Scientific Notation to Decimal Form

To convert from scientific notation back to decimal form, use the exponent to determine how many places to move the decimal point:

Converting to Decimal Form

  • Positive exponent — move the decimal point to the right that many places, filling in zeros as needed.
  • Negative exponent — move the decimal point to the left that many places, filling in zeros as needed.

Example 3

Convert \(5.45 \times 10^{5}\) to decimal form.

Solution

The exponent is positive (5), so move the decimal point 5 places to the right:

5 . 4 5 _ _ _
↓
1 ↓
2 ↓
3 ↓
4 ↓
5
Decimal moves 5 places right; fill blanks with zeros
\[5.45 \times 10^{5} = 545{,}000\]

Example 4

Convert \(6.05 \times 10^{-6}\) to decimal form.

Solution

The exponent is negative (−6), so move the decimal point 6 places to the left:

_ _ _ _ _ . 6 0 5
↑
6 ↑
5 ↑
4 ↑
3 ↑
2 ↑
1
Decimal moves 6 places left; fill blanks with zeros
\[6.05 \times 10^{-6} = 0.00000605\]

Self Check C

Write \(3.204 \times 10^{4}\) in decimal form.

Positive exponent — move decimal 4 places to the right:

\[3.204 \times 10^{4} = 32{,}040\]

Self Check D

Write \(6.789 \times 10^{-7}\) in decimal form.

Negative exponent — move decimal 7 places to the left:

\[6.789 \times 10^{-7} = 0.0000006789\]

Summary

Scientific notation expresses very large or very small numbers as a coefficient (between 1 and 10) multiplied by a power of 10. To convert to scientific notation, move the decimal point until one non-zero digit is in front — left movement gives a positive exponent, right movement gives a negative exponent. To convert back to decimal form, move the decimal point the number of places indicated by the exponent — right for positive, left for negative — filling in zeros as needed.
