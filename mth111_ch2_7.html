Chapter 2: The Language of Algebra

Section 2.7: Solving Percent Problems

Learning Objective(s)

  1. Identify the amount, the base, and the percent in a percent problem.
  2. Find the unknown in a percent problem.

Introduction

Percents are a ratio of a number and 100, which makes them easier to compare than fractions since they always have the same denominator. A store may have a 10% off sale — the amount saved is always the same portion of the price, but a higher price means more money is taken off. Interest rates on a savings account work the same way: the more money you put in, the more interest you earn. In the last section we found a percent of a whole; in this section we will expand on that.

Parts of a Percent Problem

Jeff has a coupon at the Guitar Store for 15% off any purchase of \(\$100\) or more. He wants to buy a used guitar priced at \(\$220\) and wonders how much the coupon will save him.

Problems involving percents have three quantities to work with: the percent, the amount, and the base.

  • The percent has the percent symbol (%) or the word "percent." In the problem above, 15% is the percent off the purchase price.
  • The base is the whole amount. In the problem above, the whole price of the guitar, \(\$220\), is the base.
  • The amount is the number that relates to the percent — it is always part of the whole. In the problem above, the amount is unknown: it will be the amount taken off the price.

You will return to this problem a bit later. The following examples show how to identify the three parts.

Example 1

Identify the percent, amount, and base in this problem: 30 is 20% of what number?

Solution

Percent 20% The percent is the number with the % symbol.
Base Unknown The base is the whole amount, which is not stated.
Amount 30 The amount is the portion based on the percent. 30 is a portion of another number.
Answer Percent = 20%, Amount = 30, Base = unknown

Note that this problem could be rewritten: 20% of what number is 30?

Example 2

Identify the percent, amount, and base in this problem: What percent of 30 is 3?

Solution

Percent Unknown The problem states "what percent?" so the percent is unknown.
Base 30 The base is the whole amount.
Amount 3 The amount is a portion of the whole.
Answer Percent = unknown, Amount = 3, Base = 30

Solving with Equations

Percent problems can be solved by writing equations. Since percents are fractions, finding a percent of another amount means multiplying — just as you would with fractions.

The Percent Equation

The percent of the base is the amount:

\[\text{Percent} \cdot \text{Base} = \text{Amount}\]

The unknown can be represented by a letter such as \(n\), a box \(\square\), or a question mark.

Example 3

Write an equation that represents the following problem: 30 is 20% of what number?

Solution

Rewrite. 20% of what number is 30? Rewrite in the form "percent of base is amount."
Identify parts. Percent: 20%, Base: unknown, Amount: 30
Write the equation. \(20\% \cdot n = 30\) Use \(n\) for the base, which is the unknown value.
Answer \(20\% \cdot n = 30\)

Once you have an equation, you can solve it by using the relationship between multiplication and division. Look at the pairs of facts below and notice the pattern in each row.

Multiplication Division
\(2 \cdot 3 = 6\) \(6 \div 2 = 3\)
\(8 \cdot 5 = 40\) \(40 \div 8 = 5\)
\(7 \cdot 4 = 28\) \(28 \div 7 = 4\)
\(6 \cdot 9 = 54\) \(54 \div 6 = 9\)

Multiplication and division are inverse operations — what one does to a number, the other undoes. So when you have an equation such as \(20\% \cdot n = 30\), you can divide 30 by 20% to find the unknown:

\[n = 30 \div 20\% = 30 \div 0.20 = 150\]

Example 4

What percent of 72 is 9?

Solution

Identify parts. Percent: unknown, Base: 72, Amount: 9
Write the equation. \(n \cdot 72 = 9\) Use \(n\) for the unknown percent.
Divide. \(n = 9 \div 72\) Divide to undo the multiplication of \(n\) times 72.
Simplify. \(n = 0.125\) Divide 9 by 72.
Convert to percent. \(n = 12.5\%\) Move the decimal point two places to the right.
Answer 12.5% of 72 is 9.

Estimation check: \(10\%\) of \(72 = 7.2\) and \(20\%\) of \(72 = 14.4\). Since 9 is between 7.2 and 14.4, an answer of 12.5% is reasonable.

Example 5

What is 110% of 24?

Solution

Identify parts. Percent: 110%, Base: 24, Amount: unknown
Write the equation. \(110\% \cdot 24 = n\) The amount is unknown, so use \(n\).
Convert and multiply. \(1.10 \cdot 24 = 26.4\) Write 110% as a decimal by moving the decimal point two places to the left, then multiply.
Answer 26.4 is 110% of 24.

Estimation check: 100% of 24 is 24, and 110% is a little more than 24, so 26.4 is reasonable.

Self Check A

18 is what percent of 48?

The equation is \(n \cdot 48 = 18\). Dividing gives \(n = 18 \div 48 = 0.375\). Converting to a percent: \(n = 37.5\%\).

18 is 37.5% of 48.

Self Check B

18 is 125% of what number?

125% written as a decimal is 1.25. The equation is \(1.25 \cdot n = 18\). Dividing gives \(n = 18 \div 1.25 = 14.4\).

18 is 125% of 14.4.

Solving Percent Applications

Learning Objective(s)

  1. Solve applications involving percents.
  2. Solve applications involving simple interest.

Percents have a wide variety of applications in everyday life, showing up regularly in taxes, discounts, markups, and interest rates. Let's return to the Guitar Store problem from the introduction.

Example 6

Jeff has a coupon at the Guitar Store for 15% off any purchase of \(\$100\) or more. He wants to buy a used guitar priced at \(\$220\). How much money will the coupon take off?

Solution

Simplify the problem. How much is 15% of \(\$220\)? Eliminate extra words.
Identify parts. Percent: 15%, Base: 220, Amount: \(n\)
Write the equation. \(15\% \cdot 220 = n\)
Convert and multiply. \(0.15 \cdot 220 = 33\) Convert 15% to 0.15, then multiply by 220.
Answer The coupon will take \(\$33\) off the original price.

Estimation check: \(10\%\) of \(220 = \$22\) and \(20\%\) of \(220 = \$44\). The answer, \(\$33\), is between these values, so it is reasonable.

Example 7

Evelyn bought some books at the local bookstore. Her total bill was \(\$31.50\), which included 5% tax. How much did the books cost before tax?

Solution

Reframe. 105% of what number = \(31.50\)? The cost of the books is 100%; adding 5% tax makes the total 105% of the original cost.
Identify parts. Percent: 105%, Base: \(n\), Amount: 31.50
Write the equation. \(105\% \cdot n = 31.50\)
Convert. \(1.05 \cdot n = 31.50\) Write 105% as a decimal.
Divide. \(n = 31.50 \div 1.05 = 30\) Divide to undo the multiplication.
Answer The books cost \(\$30\) before tax.

Example 8

Susana worked 20 hours at her job last week. This week, she worked 35 hours. In terms of a percent, how much more did she work this week than last week?

Solution

Simplify. 35 is what percent of 20? Eliminate extra words.
Identify parts. Percent: \(n\), Base: 20, Amount: 35
Write the equation. \(n \cdot 20 = 35\)
Divide. \(n = 35 \div 20 = 1.75\) Divide to undo the multiplication.
Convert. \(n = 175\%\) Write 1.75 as a percent.
Answer Since 35 is 175% of 20, Susana worked 75% more this week than last week. (She worked 100% of last week's hours, plus 75% more.)

Self Check C

A bookcase that was originally \(\$150\) is on sale for 15% off. What is the sale price?

Find 15% of \(\$150\): \(0.15 \cdot 150 = \$22.50\). This is the discount amount.

Subtract the discount from the original price: \(\$150 - \$22.50 = \$127.50\).

The sale price is \(\$127.50\).

Simple Interest

When a person takes out a loan, most lenders charge interest — a fee for borrowing money, typically a percent rate charged per year. We can compute simple interest by finding the interest rate percentage of the amount borrowed, then multiplying by the number of years.

Simple Interest Equation

\[I = p \cdot r \cdot t\]
  • \(I\) is the interest paid
  • \(p\) is the principal — the original amount of money borrowed
  • \(r\) is the interest rate, a per-year rate written as a decimal
  • \(t\) is the time of the loan, expressed in years or portions of a year

Example 9

Treasury Notes (T-notes) are bonds issued by the federal government to cover its expenses. Suppose you obtain a \(\$1{,}000\) T-note with a 4% annual rate, with a maturity in 2 years. How much interest will you earn?

Solution

Identify parts. \(I\) = unknown, \(p = \$1{,}000\), \(r = 4\% = 0.04\), \(t = 2\) years
Write the equation. \(I = 1{,}000 \cdot 0.04 \cdot 2\) Substitute into the simple interest formula.
Multiply. \(I = 80\)
Answer You would earn \(\$80\) in interest.

Example 10

A friend asks to borrow \(\$240\), offering to repay you \(\$250\) in 1 month. What annual interest rate is this equivalent to?

Solution

Identify parts. \(I = \$10\), \(p = \$240\), \(r\) = unknown, \(t = 1\) month Your friend pays back \(\$10\) more than borrowed, so \(I = \$10\).
Convert time. \(t = \dfrac{1}{12}\) year Convert 1 month to a fraction of a year.
Write the equation. \(10 = 240 \cdot r \cdot \dfrac{1}{12}\) Substitute into the simple interest formula.
Simplify. \(10 = r \cdot \dfrac{240}{12} = r \cdot 20\) Regroup and simplify.
Divide. \(r = 10 \div 20 = 0.50\) Divide to undo the multiplication.
Convert. \(r = 50\%\) Write 0.50 as a percent.
Answer This is equivalent to a 50% annual interest rate.

Self Check D

Find the interest on \(\$5{,}000\) borrowed at 6% for 4 years.

\[I = 5{,}000 \cdot 0.06 \cdot 4 = \$1{,}200\]

The interest is \(\$1{,}200\).

Summary

Percent problems have three parts: the percent, the base (or whole), and the amount. Any of those parts may be the unknown value. To solve percent problems, use the equation Percent · Base = Amount and solve for the unknown. When solving application problems, carefully identify which quantity is the percent, which is the base, and which is the amount before writing the equation. For simple interest problems, use the formula \(I = p \cdot r \cdot t\), making sure to express the rate as a decimal and the time in years.
